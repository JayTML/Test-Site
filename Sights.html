Sights



Mount Tammany

Mount Tammany is the southernmost peak of the Kittatinny Mountains in Knowlton Township, Warren County, New Jersey, United States. It is 1,526 feet tall, and forms the east side of the Delaware Water Gap. Across the Gap is Mount Minsi, on the Pennsylvania side of the river.


Mount Minsi
Mount Minsi forms the Pennsylvania side of the Delaware Water Gap, and is the eastern extent of the Blue Mountain. It is composed of sandstone and conglomerates of the Shawangunk Formation. The band of exposed metaquartzite cliffs on east face of Minsi hold a number of traditional climbing routes. The earliest of these were put up in 1975. Winter can offer some mixed and ice climbing as well.

Red Dot trail
The Mt. Tammany Trail Red Dot trail is one of two trails that lead to the outstanding view looking into the Delaware Water Gap from Mt. Tammany. This hike is one of the most challenging in the park. Combining the Mt. Tammany Trail Red Dot trail with the PahaquarryBlue Blaze trail that makes a three-mile loop. If making a loop hike, consider hiking up the Red Dot Trail and down the Pahaquarry Trail Blue Blaze trail, because most people find it to be an easier route.

Blue Dot trail
There are two visitor centers, one in New Jersey near the Delaware River bridge and the other in Dingman's Ferry, Pennsylvania. Headquarters is located on River Road in Pennsylvania. The New Jersey side of the gap is also protected within Worthington State Forest, a New Jersey State Forest wholly contained within the recreation area.
